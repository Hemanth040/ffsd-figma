Symptoms - Leaf Rust

Puccinia triticina • Wheat Disease Identification

When to Take Action

  • Immediate action: If flag leaf shows pustules
  • Urgent: When 5-10% leaf area is infected
  • Monitor closely: During warm humid weather
  • Critical stage: Booting to grain fill period

Quick Identification Tips

  • Look for orange-brown powdery pustules on leaves
  • Rub leaf surface - spores will stain fingers orange
  • Check both upper and lower leaf surfaces
  • Scout regularly during stem elongation to flowering

Disease Symptoms (10)

1

Small Yellow Flecks

Early

Upper leaf surface • Tillering to Stem Elongation

Tiny yellow to orange colored spots appear on leaf surface, typically 0.5-1mm in diameter. These are the earliest visible signs of infection.

Visibility: Requires close inspection
2

Orange-Brown Pustules

Moderate

Both leaf surfaces • Stem Elongation to Booting

Circular to oval raised pustules develop, containing masses of orange-brown urediniospores. Pustules are scattered randomly on leaf surface.

Visibility: Easily visible
3

Powdery Spore Mass

Moderate

Leaf surface and sheaths • Booting to Flowering

Pustules rupture the leaf epidermis, releasing dusty orange-brown spores that can be rubbed off on fingers when touched.

Visibility: Very conspicuous
4

Chlorotic Halos

Severe

Around pustules • Flowering to Grain Fill

Yellow to pale green halos form around individual pustules as the infection progresses, indicating tissue damage.

Visibility: Easily visible
5

Pustule Coalescence

Severe

Entire leaf blade • Flowering to Grain Fill

Multiple pustules merge together, covering large areas of leaf tissue and reducing photosynthetic capacity significantly.

Visibility: Very conspicuous

Weed Identification & Control Guide

Phalaris Minor

Wheat

Also Known As: Little Seed Canary Grass

Characteristics: Grass weed with narrow leaves, purple-tinged stems, and spike-like panicles
Control Methods: Pre-emergence herbicides, hand weeding, crop rotation
Critical Stage: Early growth stage (2-4 weeks after sowing)
Threat Level: High

Cyperus Rotundus

Rice

Also Known As: Purple Nut Sedge

Characteristics: Triangular stems, dark green leaves, aromatic tuberous roots
Control Methods: Post-emergence herbicides, hand weeding, flooding
Critical Stage: Before tuber formation (30-45 days)
Threat Level: High

Echinochloa Crus-Galli

Rice

Also Known As: Barnyard Grass

Characteristics: Tall grass with thick stems, large leaf blades, branching panicle
Control Methods: Pre-emergence herbicide, hand weeding, water management
Critical Stage: First 30 days after sowing
Threat Level: Medium

Parthenium Hysterophorus

Cotton

Also Known As: Carrot Grass, Congress Grass

Characteristics: Bushy plant with feathery leaves, white flower heads, rapid growth
Control Methods: Manual removal, post-emergence herbicides, competitive cropping
Critical Stage: Before flowering (45-60 days)
Threat Level: High

Avena Fatua

Wheat

Also Known As: Wild Oat

Characteristics: Tall grass with drooping panicles, flat leaves, twisted awns
Control Methods: Pre-emergence herbicides, crop rotation, seed cleaning
Critical Stage: Tillering stage (25-35 days)
Threat Level: Medium

Disease Guide - Leaf Rust

Leaf Rust

Puccinia triticina

Wheat
Severity Level High
Seasonal Risk High during humid, warm weather (Spring/Fall)

Leaf rust is one of the most common and widespread wheat diseases worldwide. It is caused by the fungus Puccinia triticina and can significantly reduce grain yield and quality if left untreated. The disease spreads rapidly under favorable conditions and can cause yield losses of 20-50% in susceptible varieties.

Disease Overview

Identification

Look for small, circular to oval-shaped, orange-brown pustules (uredinia) scattered randomly on the upper surface of leaves. As the disease progresses, pustules may also appear on leaf sheaths and stems.

Occurrence

Occurs worldwide in wheat-growing regions, particularly during periods of moderate temperatures (60-77°F) and high humidity.

Impact on Yield

Can reduce yields by 20-50% in susceptible varieties. Severe infections can also reduce grain quality and test weight.

Causes & Favorable Conditions

Pathogen

Puccinia triticina (fungus)

Favorable Conditions

  • Moderate temperatures (60-77°F / 15-25°C)
  • High relative humidity (>95%)
  • Leaf wetness for 6+ hours
  • Presence of susceptible wheat varieties
  • Prevailing wind for spore dispersal

Prevention & Control

Cultural Methods

  • Use rust-resistant wheat varieties
  • Plant early to avoid peak infection periods
  • Remove volunteer wheat plants
  • Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization
  • Ensure proper plant spacing for airflow

Chemical Control

  • Apply triazole fungicides (e.g., Propiconazole)
  • Use strobilurin fungicides (e.g., Azoxystrobin)
  • Apply at first sign of infection
  • Follow pre-harvest interval guidelines
  • Rotate fungicide modes of action

Monitoring

  • Scout fields weekly during high-risk periods
  • Check lower leaves first for early signs
  • Monitor weather forecasts for favorable conditions
  • Use disease forecasting models
  • Keep records of infection levels

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I identify leaf rust vs. stripe rust?

Leaf rust produces discrete, circular orange-brown pustules scattered on leaves. Stripe rust produces elongated pustules in stripes along the leaf. Stripe rust usually appears earlier in the season and prefers cooler temperatures.

When should I start scouting for leaf rust?

Begin scouting at the stem extension stage (Feekes 5-6) and continue through heading. Focus on the lower leaves first as infections typically start there.

Can leaf rust be completely eliminated?

No, leaf rust cannot be completely eliminated as the fungus overwinters in many regions. The goal is to manage the disease through integrated strategies to minimize economic losses.
